PROBLEM STATEMENT

Food Waste in Households Due To Poor Ingredient Management

When purchasing food ingredients, managing their shelf life is difficult and cumbersome. Also, it’s hard to track what food is left in the refrigerator, which leads to unnecessary purchases and exceeded shelf life. Both matters can ultimately cause enormous food waste problem. The food must not be wasted anymore in household.

THE FACT

In Canada, an average household wastes 140 kilogramsof food per year, which is more than $1,100 per year.

Among them, avoidable housegold food waste is 63%. All types of food are wasted, but vegetables and fruit are the most wasted foods by weight. In US, an everage person wastes 108kg of food per year at home which is about $1,800 per year. The thing is: wasting food causes energy waste. It heavily contributes to the emission of greenhouse gases. According to research, 1 tonne household food waste is the equivalent of taking one car off the road each year.

DESIGN PROCESS

USER RESEARCH - ONLINE SURVEY

I conducted a survey through google survey form. There were 5 questions and 24 people participated. The age range was from 20s to 30s.

CONCLUSION OF THE SURVEY

1:1 Interview - Meet Emily

After the user survey, I conducted 1:1 interview with Emily. She is 25 years old and living in Toronto with her roomates now. She goes to the grocery once a week or every other week. Here is some main questions that I asked:

What I learned

1. Many people don't bring a shopping list when shopping grocery.

2. Not bringing a shopping list when grocery increases the probability of buying more food than necessary.

3. Buying too much food more than you need is likely to lead to food waste.

4. People don't manage every single item in the refrigerator, so they have to throw it away without noticing that the expiration date has passed.

5. People are looking for some useful products that they don't need to input information manually.

UNDERSTAD CONSUMER'S BEHAVIOR

“People almost entirely neglect the cost of the food they’re throwing away from their kitchen” - says Victoria Ligon of the University of Arizona

I researched about consumer’s grocery behavior of buying food in bulk, and how they usually make decisions about buying food, and the following are the main reasons why they do bulk-buying:

1. They buy food in bulk because the believe that they save significant money.
2. When buying food in bulk, they do not need to worry about running out of the food.
3. Buying in bulk means that they do not need to go to the grocery store often.

However, the truth is that consumers pay more upfront to buying in bulk because they need to buy the food items all now rather than spacing out the purchase. Also, sometimes people realize later that they don’t actually like the food they bought in bulk. In this case, they will have a difficult time to finish all of it and throw away the remainder. The biggest cons of buying food in bulk is that the items will sit around past their expiration dates. Avocado will turn brown and the carrot will be slimy. It is really hard to plan their meal ahead, and the food items will go bad before being used. Otherwise, people should be friends with the refrigerator and manage food items regularly by tracking the expiry date and quantities.

MARKET RESEARCH

#1 Smart Refrigerator

There is smart refrigerators from Samsung to help user have a better experience with managing their food ingredients. Samsung refrigerators are connected to user’s smartphone via the internet. There is built-in cameras in the refrigerator. With the smartphone app, users are able to see what is inside. The expiration date of food can also be determined one by one on the screen. And it sends users the alarm when the door is left open.

Smart Refrigerator Main Features

However, not many people can afford this refrigerator because the price is high. Depending on the models it costs $2,199~$5,199. Also, users need to manually punch in the shelf life of each food on the screen to track the expiry date. Smart refrigerator have not yet become commonplace. #2 Mobile App: Beep

I found a useful app in the market which is a mobile application called Beep. With this app, users are able to track their food by simply scanning the barcode. And it gives you an alarm when the expiry date is soon. They also provide some feature that users can invite friends or family members. However, when I look at the reviews, the number of ‘1-star ratings’ were more than ‘5-stars’. The main problem was the barcode scanning does not work and users end up manually need to encode the expiration. This is because the barcodes itself do not have expiry date embedded. Most users seemed frustrated because the description of this product makes users think that it will automatically pick up the expiry date when scanning the barcode.

USER PERSONA

BRAINSTORMING

What are some cases where food is wasted at home?

1. Food Spoilage

This is caused by improper storage, lack of visibility in refrigerators, and misjudged foor needs.

2. Over Preparing:

People cook the large amount of cooks and often forget to eat leftover.

3. Date Label Confusion

Confusion between ‘sell by’, ‘best if used by’ and ‘expires by’.

4. Overbuying

Overbuy food because of advertisement and sales promotion.

5. Poor planning

Consumers supposed to plan their meal plan ahead and make a shopping list when grocery.

What can be done to stop food waste at home?

1. Buy food with a meal plan and shopping list.

2. Store food correctly.

3. Check the expiration date carefully and should not leave the food ingredients until they go bad.

4. Not buy food in bulk. In reality, consumers are saving more money when they don’t buy food in bulk.

What product can be made to prevent food waste at home?

Smart refrigerator is one of the options that consumers can prevent from food waste. However, the price is very high for consumers to afford. A mobile app that manage meal and food ingredients can be more convenient and useful for consumers to use casually. In that app, there can be some features like:

1. Managing shelf life of the food with the notification

2. Storage tips

3. View and manage food by creating multiple refrigerator

4. Meal plan note

THE SOLUTION

Finding ways to reduce food waste at home on daily basis can make a big difference and help prevent issues contributing to more environmental problem. To solve this problem, a food tracking app can be made which helps consumers avoid buying too much, make a shopping list, store food correctly, track the inventory and expiry date. For example, many consumers are not sure how to store fruits and vegetable correctly and eventually food go bad before consumers use it for cooking. With storage tip feature, they able to store food correctly. For example, tomatoes and onions should not be refrigerated. They also can shop smart with the meal plan note. Meal plan note will let them plan their weekly meal. This can help them to avoid buying more food than they need. Consumers can check shelf life and inventory easily too. The features in this app will prevent wasting food and their money. If individual reduce food waste, it will greatly affect the environment in a positive way.

What is the biggest challenge when creating an app and what is the solution?

The biggest challenge when creating a food tracking app is to create the app that users don’t need to manually punch in all food they buy. It must be frustrating and hassle if users manually type it. The thing is that the barcode scanning does not enter the expiry date automatically since the information is not embedded in the barcode. My solution is that using an A.I technology, when consumers take a photo of a receipt, products are all entered automatically by scanning. Then, the next step is taking pictures of the ‘best before’ on the food package, and then the app will read it automatically too. With this method, users are able to greatly save their time as it does not require any manual typing.
